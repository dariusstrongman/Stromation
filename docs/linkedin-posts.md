## Week 3: "Why most businesses fail at automation"

Ive seen a lot of automation projects fail. Heres what usually goes wrong.

**They automate a broken process.** If your workflow doesnt make sense when a human does it, it wont make sense when a machine does it either. Fix the process first.

**They start too big.** Dont try to automate your entire business in one shot. Pick the one task thats eating the most time, automate that, prove it works, then expand.

**They pick the wrong tool.** Zapier is great for simple connections. But if your workflow has conditional logic, multiple branches, error handling needs, or touches more than 3 tools, you probably need something like n8n or a custom build.

**They dont build in error handling.** The automation that breaks silently is worse than no automation at all. If something fails, you need to know about it immediately.

**They dont document anything.** Six months later nobody remembers how it works, nobody can change it, and it becomes a black box everyone is afraid to touch.

The businesses that succeed with automation treat it like infrastructure, not a magic trick. Build it right, monitor it, and iterate.
